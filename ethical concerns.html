Legal and Ethical Concerns

Guidance and information for dealing with Legal and Ethical dilemmas prior to and during Testing

This section will provide resources available to give guidance on what ethical concerns need to be considered during testing
No specific legal or ethical advice will be given, due to the specific indivudual nature of each penetration test.

Prior to conducting a Social Engineering Penetration test, the tester and client need to discuss in depth methods which are disallowed from
being used, along with aspects of the business which are off-limits for the tester to penetrate. This would be covered in the scope discussion
and ensures that the tester does not attempt to penetrate any third party companies accidentaly. Third party companies are commonly used
by businesses for services, such as cleaning and server hosting. If a tester accidently penetrated one of these companies, it could lead to law
suits being broguht toward the tester due to those third party companies not providing explicit concent to test their services.

Due to the varying nature of laws in different countries and states, it is considered best practice to seek legal advice within the country or state you wish to test prior to,
or during the initial client meeting, this ensures that all aspects are considered and the tester is acting on the appropriate side of the law during the test. This also ensures
that the client is aware of the laws and is on board with the type of test and scope which is considered.
Guidance on the UK stance for computer related misuse and use:Computer Misuse Act 1990
Guidance on the United States of America stance for cyber crime and computer misuse: Computer Fraud and Abuse Act of 1986

Regarding ethics, a framework for this has been proposed by "www.Social-Engineer.org" CEO Chris Hadnagy. His proposed ethics codes is constructed
of 11 points which help guide fellow professionals to keep them to a proposed industry standard, based off the idea of "leave them feeling better for having met you".
