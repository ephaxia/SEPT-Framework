Establishing Relationships & rapport with target

Guidance on forming a relationship with a target, leading to successful exploitation

Prior to any form of exploitation taking place, an initial relationship needs to be formed with the target (depending on exploit).
This obviously depends on what form of communication method which is going to be used, for example, spear phishing, or in person, verbal communication.
Once information has been gathered about a target, and an attack vector has been decided upon (dictated by scope and client requirements),
communication and rapport building can begin.

Mouton et al. (2016) establish some compliance principles which will aid you when starting communication with a target and beginning to build rapport.

  1. Friendship or liking: People are more willing to comply with requests from friends or people they like.
  2. Commitment or consistency: Once committed to something, people are more willing to comply with requests consistent with this position.
  3. Scarcity: People are more willing to comply with requests that are scarce or decreasing in availability.
  4. Reciprocity: People are more willing to comply with a request if the requester has treated them favourably in the past
  5. Social Validation: People are more willing to comply with a request if it is seen as the socially correct thing to do.
  6. Authority: People easily comply with requests received from people with more authority than they have.
Using these complicance principles can aid in developing a relationship which results in successful exploitation. Understandably some principles rely on
the tester maintaining a fictional position higher than the target, however, this is possible through impersonisation, whether that be through email, or in person
by using a fictional 3rd party supplier character.
