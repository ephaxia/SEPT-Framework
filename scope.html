Scope Discussion

Discussing scope of a SEPT with a client & possible exclusions

When discussing scope with a client prior to conducting a SEPT, a few important considerations need to be noted and accounted for:

  • Does the client use any third party suppliers for services?
  • Does the client want the whole organisation tested, or specific departments/facillities?
  • Does the client want a test just on site? Just off-site? Or a mixture of both?
  • What is off limits in terms of exploitation techniques?
  • "Get out of jail free card" (required if security or law enforcement approaches the tester)
  • Will any information be provided on system or systems being tested? (white box or black box testing)
  • Is technical exploitation warranted as a combination attack?

These points need to be carefully considered with the client, and discussed in depth prior to commencing.
Another item to note is ethical and legal considerations (see Ethical & Legal concerns for further guidance on this).
These also need to be discussed with the client, as to what exploitation methods they will allow against their
employees. Does any employee pose a risk in terms of mental health, or possible stress issues. Once these
items are carefully considered, and a scope is decided upon, the tester can move to the next phase of the SEPT
lifecycle, Information gathering.
