Information Gathering and OSINT (Open Source Intellegence)

Tools and guidance on how to conduct information gathering prior to exploit creation

Prior to conducting any exploitation, information needs to be gathered about the target, whether that be the company, or a specific employee.
The best approach is to find as much publically available information about the targets as possible, prior to engagement. This can be conducted
through mutliple different methods, ranging from physical observation, phone calls, emails and OSINT (Open Source Intellegence).

OSINT
This refers to information which is publically available, such as information posted by employees or the company on their own websites, or on
social media websites (e.g. Facebook, Twitter, Instagram). OSINT is a resource which can be incredibly useful when finding information needed to
formulate an attack, such information as employee badge types can often be found in photos, as well as company heirarchy and contact emails.

A tool has already been created to help professionals conduct OSINT investigations, this tool can be found at OSINTframework
This tool is created in the United states, so some sites may not be applicable for those working in other countries. Use your discretion to filter out what is beneficial.
